# RiftbornAI Level Design School

**Read this track before creating or editing maps, blockouts, arenas, corridors, room layouts, traversal routes, or dungeon spaces.**

Level design is not just geometry. It is player decision-making in space.

Good level design:

- tells the player where to look and where to go
- balances exposure, safety, speed, and choice
- uses landmarks and sightlines to reduce confusion
- proves layout through play before committing to heavy art pass

Bad level design can still look impressive in screenshots while playing poorly.

## Core Rules

- Block out gameplay space before polishing visual detail.
- Every route needs a purpose: critical path, flank, retreat, reward, or pacing change.
- Landmarks and lighting should help orientation, not merely decorate.
- Validate traversal and combat space in motion, not only from editor stills.
- Navigation, cover spacing, and sightlines are part of the design, not cleanup after art.
